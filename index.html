About SeKO

A bridge between two homes

Somalische Europäische Kooperation e.V. (SeKO) was founded in Worms, Germany to support Somali families and individuals in building a stable, dignified life — while staying connected to their culture and heritage.

We work with families at every stage: from their first days in Germany through to education, employment, and full community participation. We are a registered non-profit (eingetragener Verein) and are independent, transparent, and accountable to the communities we serve.

Why we exist

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To empower Somali families and individuals living in Germany with practical support, education, and advocacy — so that every person can participate fully and confidently in German society without losing their cultural identity.

Our Vision

A Germany where Somali communities are visible, valued, and self-determining — and where cultural heritage enriches rather than excludes. We believe cooperation between two worlds creates something stronger than either alone.

What we do

Practical support at every step

Six areas of work — built around what families actually need, in the languages they speak.

Integration & Settlement

Help with registration, housing, schools and finding your feet in a new city.

Education & Language

German and Somali language courses, homework clubs and adult learning programs.

Youth Programs

Sport, mentoring and weekend activities that keep young people connected.

Legal & Administrative Help

One-to-one guidance with paperwork, appointments and official letters.

Advocacy & Representation

Standing up for the community's voice with local and federal institutions.

Community & Culture

Eid celebrations, family days and cultural events that bring us together.
